Addition definition math - what is an addend?

Summation, also called addition, is the process of calculating the total of two (or more) addends. What is an addend? Those numbers that are being added together are called addends, which, in our calculator, are marked as A and B. The result of this process is called a sum, in this addition calculator known as C.

The addition has two properties: commutativity and associativity. Commutativity means that you can exchange the places of the addends without changing the result of the equation,

A + B = B + A.

Associativity, on the other hand, becomes a thing once we try to add more than two numbers. It means it doesn't matter whether you add the first number to the second and then the third or third to the second and then the first. The result won't change, A + (B + C) = (A + B) + C.
